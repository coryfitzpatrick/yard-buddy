# Bermuda Drought Management — Phoenix AZ (Zone 9b–10a)

Common and hybrid bermuda are dominant warm-season turfgrasses in Phoenix and the desert Southwest. Drought management here is fundamentally different from humid Southeast bermuda.

## Weekly irrigation target (CRITICAL)
- **Phoenix AZ summer bermuda irrigation: 1–2 INCHES PER WEEK total** (University of Arizona Cooperative Extension range for established bermuda in Phoenix summer).
  - **Mild summer (90–100°F daytime):** 1–1.5 inches/week
  - **Peak summer (100–110°F daytime):** 1.5–2 inches/week
  - **Extreme heat (>110°F sustained):** up to 2 inches/week with cycle-and-soak
- This is the TOTAL across all sessions per week, NOT per-session.
- DO NOT recommend 1.5–2 inches per SESSION — totaling 4.5–6 inches per week causes runoff even in hot/dry conditions.
- DO NOT recommend 2.5+ inches per WEEK in any conditions — that exceeds even peak summer extension guidance.

## Application technique — cycle-and-soak
- Use cycle-and-soak across MULTIPLE sessions per week (3 sessions typical)
- Each session: 0.3–0.5 inches delivered via multiple short cycles to allow infiltration
- Example: 3 sessions of ~0.4 inches each = 1.2 inches/week total
- Time of day: BEFORE 10 AM to minimize evaporation
- For desert clay/caliche soils with poor infiltration: shorter cycles (5–8 min) with longer soak intervals (45–60 min between)

## Drought stress signs
- Footprinting: footprints remain visible after walking on turf — water now
- Blade folding/wilting: bermuda blades fold to reduce surface area — water now
- Bluish-gray cast across the lawn — water now
- Color loss to straw/tan — beyond simple drought, may indicate dormancy onset

## Mowing height for Phoenix bermuda
- **Common bermuda Phoenix, variety unknown: 1.5–2 inches** (safe guideline for drought stress management; covers most common bermuda).
- **Hybrid/dwarf varieties (confirmed):** 0.5–1.5 inches. Common AZ hybrids include Tifway 419, MidIron, Princess 77 (most prefer reel mowing).
- **When recommending mowing height, ALWAYS note cultivar uncertainty:** "If your bermuda is hybrid (e.g., Tifway, MidIron), aim for 0.75–1.5 inches; for common bermuda or unknown cultivar, 1.5–2 inches is safer."
- Do NOT apply UGA common bermuda range (1.5–2.5") as default for all AZ bermuda without acknowledging variety uncertainty.
- During drought stress: RAISE to upper end of cultivar's range. Never LOWER height during drought — taller grass shades soil and reduces water loss.

## Fertilization during drought
- DO NOT recommend fertilization of any kind during active drought stress — applying fertilizer to drought-stressed turf causes salt burn and amplifies stress.
- Defer fertilization until turf has recovered (2–3 weeks of normal growth).

## Wetting agents and humectants
- Hydretain is a humectant/moisture-retention product — captures and holds soil moisture. NOT a surfactant wetting agent.
- For HYDROPHOBIC SOIL (water beading on surface, refusing to absorb): a penetrant surfactant wetting agent helps. Products: Revolution, Aqua-Aid Penetrant, generic penetrant wetting agents labeled for turf.
- DO NOT cite Hydretain irrigation reduction percentages — manufacturer efficiency claims have no extension validation. Use ONLY phrasing like "may help with moisture retention." Never "reduces irrigation by X%."

## NO fungicide during drought
- Dry conditions (low humidity, no rainfall) prevent fungal disease development
- Any fungicide recommendation during active drought is agronomically incorrect
- Defer fungicide topics until moisture conditions return

## Avoid these products on stressed bermuda
- **NEVER recommend Tenacity (mesotrione) for bermuda** — phytotoxic, causes severe bleaching and damage; not labeled for bermuda
- Do NOT recommend pre-emergent herbicides during peak drought (no germination occurring); plan ahead for fall application instead

## Bermuda spring-summer fertilization (when NOT in drought stress)
- Begin N when soil temp consistently >65°F (full green-up)
- Rate: 0.5–1.0 lbs N per 1,000 sq ft per application, every 4–6 weeks during growing season
- Total annual N for AZ bermuda: 2–4 lbs N per 1,000 sq ft (UoA Extension range)
- Product: balanced N source (urea, polymer-coated urea, or 16-8-8 type blends)
