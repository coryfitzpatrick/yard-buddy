# Soil Temperature Timing Reference

Soil temperature at 2-inch depth is the primary timing trigger for most turf management decisions — significantly more reliable than calendar dates or air temperature.

## Crabgrass pre-emergent timing
- **Standard trigger: 55°F at 2-inch depth, maintained 3 consecutive days** (Penn State, Purdue, K-State consensus)
- Early-start option for heavy pressure: 50–53°F provides buffer in fast-warming springs
- Frame in recommendations: "The standard trigger is 55°F; applying at 50–53°F provides a buffer in fast-warming springs."
- CRITICAL: If current soil temp is already 55–60°F, the window has PASSED or is CLOSING — do NOT frame 60°F as "ideal window"
- Above 60°F, crabgrass germination may be underway — pivot to post-emergent options (quinclorac, Drive XLG) if crabgrass already visible

## Winter annual pre-emergent timing (Poa annua, chickweed, henbit)
- **Trigger: 70°F at 2-inch depth, FALLING** — soil cooling through 70°F in fall captures the early germination window
- Apply when soil temps reach the upper end of the Poa annua germination range
- Do NOT wait until soil reaches 55°F — by then early germination may have begun in heavy pressure lawns
- Typical calendar: late September to mid-October in zones 5–7; October in zones 8–9

## Cool-season overseeding (KBG, tall fescue, fine fescue, perennial ryegrass)
- **Optimal soil temp: 60–65°F** for fast, reliable germination
- This is the late summer / early fall target window (typically late August through mid-September in zones 5–7)
- Acceptable range: 50–65°F. Below 55°F, germination slows significantly; seedlings may not establish before winter dormancy.
- Above 70°F, cool-season seed germination rate drops sharply (heat stress)
- DO NOT frame 50°F as "ideal" — 60–65°F is optimal

## Warm-season green-up fertilization
- Soil temp sustained 65°F at 2-inch depth before applying N to bermuda, zoysia, St. Augustine, centipede
- Below 65°F: dormant or transitioning turf cannot uptake nutrients; N at this stage promotes weed germination and disease

## Soil temperature resources by state
- **Colorado:** CoAgMet (Colorado Agricultural Meteorological Network) — coagmet.colostate.edu. CSU Extension-verified, Colorado-only. Do NOT recommend for non-Colorado states.
- **Other states:** state Cooperative Extension's soil temperature network, or a calibrated soil thermometer at 2-inch depth
- DO NOT recommend SoilTemperature.org for Colorado users (not CSU-verified)
- ALWAYS instruct the homeowner to verify soil temp — do NOT infer from air temperature alone (can differ 10–20°F)

## High-altitude / high-desert climates (Denver, Salt Lake City, Albuquerque)
- Spring soil temps LAG air temps by 3–6 weeks
- Even when air temp reaches 65–70°F in April, soil at 2-inch depth may still be 45–50°F
- DO NOT recommend "urgent" spring pre-emergent based on air temp alone in these climates
- Wait for actual soil thermometer reading before applying

## Granular pre-emergent activation
All granular pre-emergents require 0.5 inches of water (rain or irrigation) to activate the barrier:
- Prodiamine (Barricade): water in within 3–5 days
- Pendimethalin (Scotts Halts): water in within 14 days per label
- Dithiopyr (Dimension): water in within 7 days
- Isoxaben (Gallery): water in within 21 days

ALWAYS include the water-in requirement when recommending a granular pre-emergent.
