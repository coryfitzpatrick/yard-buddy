# EDDHA Chelated Iron for Alkaline Soils

EDDHA (ethylene-diamine-di(o-hydroxyphenyl)acetic acid) is the chelate of choice for delivering plant-available iron to turf growing on alkaline soils (pH > 7.0).

## Why EDDHA vs other chelates
| Chelate | Effective pH range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **EDDHA** | up to ~pH 9.0 | Best choice for alkaline soils. Maintains iron in plant-available form even at pH 8+ |
| EDTA | up to ~pH 6.5 | Loses effectiveness above 7.0; largely ineffective above 7.5. NEVER recommend as equivalent to EDDHA for alkaline soils. |
| DTPA | up to ~pH 7.5 | Intermediate; suitable for mildly alkaline soils |
| Iron sulfate | <pH 6.5 | Inorganic; precipitates and becomes unavailable above pH 6.5 |
| Iron oxide (Ironite) | <pH 6.5 | Minimally plant-available at pH > 6.5 |

## EDDHA retail products
- **Sequestrene 138 Fe** — 6% iron, the standard EDDHA chelate product
- **Sprint 138** — 6% iron EDDHA, equivalent
- **Greenway Biotech EDDHA 6%**
- Generic agricultural EDDHA chelates labeled "Fe-EDDHA"

## Application method (CRITICAL)
EDDHA chelated iron is **SOIL-APPLIED, NOT FOLIAR**. The chelate works by keeping iron in plant-available form in the root zone — it must be watered into the soil to reach roots.
- Apply granular product to turf
- Water in with at least 0.25 inches of irrigation
- Foliar application is INEFFECTIVE for EDDHA — the chelate does not penetrate leaf cuticle efficiently

For quick cosmetic foliar greening (short-lived only), iron sulfate spray at 2 oz/gallon water can be used — but this is NOT a long-term fix and lasts only 7–14 days.

## Response timeline
- Mild deficiency at pH 7.5–7.8: new growth greens up in 10–14 days
- Moderate deficiency: 2–3 weeks for visible improvement; 4–6 weeks for full recovery
- Severe deficiency at pH >8.0: 4–8 weeks for visible response

DO NOT cite "3–6 months" for visible response to EDDHA iron — that timeline applies to soil pH correction with sulfur, NOT to chelated iron greening.

## When to recommend EDDHA iron
- Visible iron chlorosis symptoms: interveinal yellowing (yellow leaves with green veins) in new growth
- Soil pH ≥ 7.5 with marginal turf color
- For pH 7.0–7.5 with no visible symptoms: monitor first; recommend only if chlorosis appears

DO NOT recommend EDDHA as "urgent" when the homeowner's profile does not describe visible chlorosis. At pH 7.8, iron deficiency is common but not guaranteed.

## Pair with long-term soil pH correction
EDDHA iron addresses SYMPTOMS, not the underlying high pH. For sustained color and overall turf health, pair with:
1. Elemental sulfur program (1–2 lbs/1,000 sq ft every 4–6 weeks)
2. Acidifying nitrogen source (ammonium sulfate 21-0-0) for ongoing fertilization
3. Soil pH retest in 3–6 months
