# Pre-Emergent Herbicide Product Catalog (Homeowner Retail)

Correct active ingredient and product mapping for pre-emergent herbicides commonly sold at retail. Always pair active ingredient with brand name when recommending — homeowners shop by brand.

## Prodiamine (Group 3 mitotic inhibitor)
- **Andersons Barricade 0.5G** (granular, 0.5% AI) — typical label rate 3.2–4.6 lbs product per 1,000 sq ft for crabgrass control (corresponds to ~0.65 lb AI/acre)
- **Quali-Pro Prodiamine 65 WDG** — wettable dispersible granule, professional sizes
- **Syngenta Barricade 65 WDG** — same active, professional formulation
- Best-in-class residual; effective on crabgrass, Poa annua, goosegrass

## Pendimethalin (Group 3)
- **Scotts Halts Crabgrass Preventer** (NOT prodiamine; commonly miscategorized)
- **Pendulum 2G granular** (Lesco/professional)
- **Scotts Turf Builder Halts Crabgrass Preventer** (pre-emergent only; do NOT confuse with Triple Action which adds broadleaf killer)

## Dithiopyr (Group 3)
- **Bonide Crabgrass & Weed Preventer** (dithiopyr)
- **Dimension 2EW** (liquid pro)
- **Greenview Crabgrass Preventer with Dimension**
- Has some early post-emergent activity on young crabgrass (up to 1-tiller stage) — unique among Group 3s

## Isoxaben (Group 21 — BROADLEAF pre-emergent)
- **Gallery 75 DF** — water-dispersible granule (WG/DF formulation). The standard professional application is as a LIQUID SUSPENSION sprayed via boom or backpack sprayer. Some homeowner applications spread the granules with a calibrated rotary spreader, but liquid application provides more uniform coverage. When recommending Gallery 75 DF, prefer the liquid suspension application method.
- **Quali-Pro Isoxaben 75 WG** — same active, professional sizing
- Critical: isoxaben targets BROADLEAF winter annuals (chickweed, henbit, bittercress). Prodiamine/pendimethalin/dithiopyr have LIMITED chickweed efficacy. For combined Poa annua + chickweed pressure, use both classes or a combination product.

## Siduron (Group 3 — overseed-safe)
- **Tupersan** — only pre-emergent that is safe to apply AT TIME OF SEEDING for cool-season grass establishment

## NOT Pre-Emergents (commonly miscategorized retail products)
- **Spectracide Weed Stop / Spectracide Weed & Grass Killer**: POST-EMERGENT broadleaf herbicide (2,4-D + dicamba + mecoprop + carfentrazone). Kills emerged broadleaf weeds. NOT a pre-emergent.
- **Spectracide Weed & Grass Killer Concentrate**: contains diquat — POST-EMERGENT non-selective.
- **Roundup / Roundup For Lawns**: glyphosate (non-selective post-emergent). "Roundup For Lawns" is a different reformulated product but still POST-EMERGENT, contains MCPA + sulfentrazone + others.
- **Ortho Weed-B-Gon**: POST-EMERGENT three-way broadleaf.
- **Scotts Turf Builder Triple Action**: pre-emergent (pendimethalin) PLUS post-emergent broadleaf; not pure pre-emergent.
- **Image Kills Nutsedge**: POST-EMERGENT for nutsedge (imazaquin).

## Activation requirements
All granular pre-emergents require 0.5 inches of water (rain or irrigation) to activate the barrier. Activation timing window varies:
- Prodiamine (Barricade): within 3–5 days of application
- Pendimethalin (Halts): within 14 days per label
- Dithiopyr (Dimension): within 7 days
- Isoxaben (Gallery): within 21 days

If no rain forecast within the activation window, instruct the homeowner to irrigate 0.5 inches manually.
