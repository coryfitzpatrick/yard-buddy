# Zoysia Spring Green-Up and Maintenance (UGA Extension)

Zoysiagrass is a warm-season grass widely used in zones 7–9. Spring transition management is the most timing-sensitive period of the year.

## Spring fertilization trigger (CRITICAL)
- **Do NOT apply nitrogen to zoysia until BOTH:**
  - Soil temperatures consistently exceed 65°F (UGA Extension standard)
  - The lawn is at least 75% green-up
- Applying N before this threshold:
  - Promotes disease (large patch flares)
  - Wastes fertilizer on dormant tissue that cannot uptake
  - Can stimulate weeds before zoysia is competitive

ALWAYS explicitly anchor the fertilization delay to the 65°F soil temperature benchmark. Do NOT give a calendar date alone without the soil temp qualifier.

## Spring disease priority — LARGE PATCH first
- **Primary spring disease: Large patch (Rhizoctonia solani AG2-2 LP)**
- Conditions: cool wet spring (55–75°F)
- Symptoms: circular dying patches with orange/yellow halo at active edge
- Treatment: azoxystrobin (Scotts DiseaseEx) or propiconazole — apply preventively in fall and curatively in spring when patches visible

- **Dollar spot (Clarireedia jacksonii)** is primarily a MID-TO-LATE SUMMER disease on zoysia in warm/humid conditions
- DO NOT list dollar spot as a spring priority alongside large patch
- In spring, focus on large patch monitoring and curative fungicide if circular dying patches visible

## Mowing height by variety
- **Fine-leaf types (Zeon, Emerald, Cavalier):** 0.5–1.5 inches (reel-mow preferred for lowest heights)
- **Coarser home-lawn types (Meyer, Z-52, Zenith, Empire):** 1.5–2.5 inches
- **Variety unknown: 1.5–2 inches** as a safe general guideline
- NEVER recommend below 1 inch unless homeowner confirms a fine-leaf variety

## Spring scalping (controversial)
- Some homeowners scalp zoysia to ~1 inch in early spring to remove dead material and promote earlier green-up
- UGA cautions: only scalp if HEALTHY zoysia stand; risks weed encroachment in thin lawns
- Standard recommendation: skip scalping unless variety confirmed and stand healthy
- Better alternative: thorough core aeration AFTER full green-up (soil >65°F, >75% green)

## Spring herbicide rules
- **MSM Turf (metsulfuron-methyl) on zoysia during spring transition: DO NOT apply**
  - Metsulfuron on partially-greened zoysia (<75% green) risks phytotoxicity and uneven discoloration
  - Defer to when zoysia is fully established and actively growing (soil temps consistently >70°F, turf fully green)
- **Three-way broadleaf products (2,4-D + MCPP + dicamba):**
  - Per UGA, wait for daytime temperatures CONSISTENTLY above 60°F
  - DO NOT apply to partially dormant/emerging turf — risks phytotoxicity and transient bleaching
  - Apply only when zoysia is at least 50% green and actively growing

## Pre-emergent for zoysia
- Spring crabgrass pre-emergent: apply at 50–55°F soil temp standard window
- This is BEFORE green-up — apply to dormant or transitioning turf
- Fall winter annual pre-emergent: 70°F soil falling

## Birmingham AL reference (Zone 8a)
- Birmingham AL (ZIP 35201–35299): Zone 8a
- Zoysia green-up typically begins mid-March to early April when soil temps reach 60°F
- Full green-up at 65°F+
- Spring fertilization window: typically late April / early May once 65°F + 75% green achieved

## Irrigation during spring green-up
- DO NOT recommend resuming irrigation during early green-up unless soil moisture is actively dry
- Early spring rainfall (0.3–0.5 inches with cool temps 60–65°F) does NOT need supplemental irrigation
- Reserve irrigation recommendations for when lawn is established and soil temps consistently above 65°F
- Avoid morning irrigation creating prolonged leaf wetness — large patch thrives in moisture

## Summer maintenance — annual N program
- Total annual N: 1–3 lbs N per 1,000 sq ft (UGA range; lower for low-maintenance, higher for high-input)
- Split into 2–4 applications during active growing season (June–August)
- Slow-release sources preferred
