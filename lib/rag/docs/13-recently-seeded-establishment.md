# Recently Seeded / Establishing Cool-Season Lawn Care

When a cool-season lawn was recently seeded or is actively germinating (within the past 6 weeks), the management approach differs sharply from established turf.

## Hard prohibitions on new seedings
- **NO pre-emergent herbicides.** Pre-emergent prevents ALL seed germination — kills your investment immediately.
  - EXCEPTION: Siduron (Tupersan) is the only pre-emergent safe at seeding time
- **NO post-emergent herbicides for at least 4 weeks after germination** (6–8 weeks is safer)
  - Most post-emergent labels require turf to be mowed at least 2–3 times before application
- **NO high-nitrogen maintenance fertilizer yet.** Seedlings need to establish first.
  - Wait until 2–3 mowings at full height (typically 6–8 weeks post-seeding) before beginning regular fertilization
- **NO combination fertilizer+insecticide products.** Scotts SummerGuard, Scotts GrubEx, or any combo product — the insecticide component may harm establishing seedlings.

## Starter fertilizer — TIMING IS NARROW
Starter fertilizer (high phosphorus: 12-24-12, 18-24-12, 24-25-4) is appropriate AT THE TIME OF SEEDING — day 0 to day 3 maximum.

**If seeding occurred MORE THAN 1 WEEK AGO or seeds are actively germinating:**
- The optimal starter window has PASSED
- Do NOT recommend applying starter fertilizer now
- Focus on germination care (moisture management) instead

After full establishment (2–3 mowings at full height, typically 6–8 weeks post-seeding), a light maintenance fertilizer (0.5 lbs slow-release N/1000 sqft) can begin the regular program.

## Watering during germination
- LIGHT AND FREQUENT — brief cycles 2–3 times daily
- Goal: keep the seed bed consistently moist; never let surface dry between cycles
- Each cycle: 5–10 minutes per zone, just enough to wet top 1 inch
- **DO NOT recommend deep infrequent irrigation** during germination — allows surface to dry, kills germinating seed
- The "before 10 AM only" rule for established lawns DOES NOT apply to new seedings — multiple daily cycles including midday are essential

## First mowing
- Begin mowing when grass reaches 3–4 inches tall
- Cut to ~2.5–3 inches initially (do not exceed 1/3 rule)
- Make sure mower blades are SHARP — dull blades tear young seedlings
- Soil should be firm (not soft/muddy) before first mow — wait for dry surface

## Disease monitoring on new seedings
Damping-off is the primary disease concern, but management differs by temperature:

**At soil temps below 75°F (typical fall overseeding 55–70°F):**
- Damping-off risk is generally LOW
- DO NOT cite Pythium blight or Pythium aphanidermatum — these are warm-season pathogens (require >85–90°F soil temp)
- If mentioning disease at all: "cool-season Pythium damping-off monitoring only if conditions stay persistently wet"
- Preventive fungicide is NOT standard university extension guidance for fall overseedings at moderate temps

**At soil temps above 75°F (late summer / hot conditions):**
- Pythium blight risk increases on new seedlings in wet conditions
- Mefenoxam (Subdue MAXX, Hi-Yield Turf Fungicide) is the appropriate Pythium treatment if damping-off symptoms appear
- Rhizoctonia damping-off treated with azoxystrobin or PCNB — different pathogens, different treatments

**DO NOT recommend preventive fungicide for damping-off** unless:
- Humidity is elevated (>70%)
- Temperatures are warm (>70°F)
- Conditions warrant it (recent rainfall, persistent moisture)

## Pythium species accuracy
- **Pythium aphanidermatum** (primary Pythium blight pathogen) is WARM-SEASON — requires air/soil temps >85–90°F
- DO NOT cite Pythium aphanidermatum as a fall overseeding risk at moderate temperatures (55–75°F)
- At fall temperatures, cool-season Pythium species (P. ultimum, P. irregulare) can cause damping-off but are far less virulent
- Use accurate species terminology: "cool-season Pythium damping-off" not "Pythium blight" for fall scenarios

## Pre-emergent planning for AFTER establishment
- If planning future pre-emergent applications, ALWAYS include soil temperature trigger
- Example phrasing: "after lawn is established (6–8 weeks post-seed, 2–3 mowings at full height), apply pre-emergent for crabgrass prevention when soil temps reach 50–55°F next spring"
- Don't just say "apply pre-emergent in spring" — be specific about timing AND establishment prerequisites

## Establishment timeline reference
| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| 0 | Seed + starter fertilizer (high P) |
| 0–7 | Multiple light irrigations daily; protect from foot traffic |
| 7–14 | Germination visible (varies by species; PRG fastest, KBG slowest) |
| 21–35 | First mowing when grass reaches 3–4 inches |
| 35–60 | Continue mowing; reduce irrigation toward deep-infrequent |
| 60+ | Full establishment; begin regular fertilization program |
