# White Grub Control — Curative vs Preventive Distinction

White grubs (larvae of Japanese beetles, masked chafers, June beetles, European chafers) are the most damaging soil-dwelling turf pests in zones 5–7. Treatment timing depends ENTIRELY on whether the situation is preventive or curative — wrong product for wrong stage = wasted application.

## Identification — confirm BEFORE treatment
- Cut a 1 sq ft × 2 inch deep flap of turf with a shovel
- Count C-shaped white larvae in the root zone
- Identify by size and raster pattern at base of abdomen
- Common species in Midwest/Northeast: Japanese beetle, masked chafer, European chafer

## Action threshold for Kentucky bluegrass
- **5–6 grubs per square foot** (many Midwest extension sources including Purdue E-75-W)
- 8–10 grubs/sq ft is on the high end of what's published; using it as the threshold may delay treatment unnecessarily
- For high-value or irrigated turf, the threshold can be lower (3–5/sq ft)
- For drought-stressed turf showing damage: even lower threshold appropriate

## CURATIVE products (use for ACTIVE grub damage / late-instar grubs already present)
- **Trichlorfon (Dylox)** — fast-acting, contact-and-ingestion. The standard curative product.
  - Apply when grubs are confirmed in top 2–3 inches of soil
  - Water in with 0.5 inch within 24 hours
  - Effective on large/late-instar grubs (3rd instar)
- **Carbaryl (Sevin)** — effective alternative to trichlorfon
  - Same application + water-in requirements

**Use ONLY these for active grub damage.** Do NOT recommend chlorantraniliprole or imidacloprid for active damage — they are preventive products with no curative effect on late-instar grubs.

## PREVENTIVE products (apply BEFORE egg hatch)
Apply May–July before egg hatch:
- **Chlorantraniliprole (Scotts GrubEx1, GrubEx, Acelepryn)** — PREVENTIVE ONLY
  - Long residual; targets early-instar grubs as they hatch
  - DOES NOT effectively control large or late-instar grubs already present
  - NEVER recommend as a curative treatment for active grub damage
- **Imidacloprid (Bayer Season-Long Grub Control, Merit, GrubEx with imidacloprid)** — PREVENTIVE
  - Similar May–July application window
  - Soil systemic; controls early-instar grubs

## Sequencing — curative + preventive follow-up
When active grub damage is visible mid-season:
1. **NOW**: Apply trichlorfon or carbaryl to control the active infestation
2. **FOLLOW-UP next May–July**: Apply preventive chlorantraniliprole or imidacloprid before the next generation's egg hatch
3. Continue preventive in subsequent years if grub pressure persists in the area

This sequence avoids the common mistake of recommending GrubEx for active damage — that's just feeding eggs that haven't hatched yet.

## Recovery overseeding after grub damage
- Wait until grubs are controlled (1–2 weeks after curative treatment) before overseeding bare patches
- Soil temp must support germination (60–65°F for cool-season)
- Damaged areas often need extensive raking to provide seed-to-soil contact
- Apply starter fertilizer at seeding (high-P 12-24-12 or 18-24-12)
- Do NOT apply pre-emergent in damaged/seeded areas — will prevent overseeding
- Fall pre-emergent timing may CONFLICT with new seedling establishment — acknowledge timing tension and prioritize seeding success over pre-emergent for the immediate season

## Starter fertilizer accuracy
When recommending starter fertilizer for grub-damaged repair areas:
- Use a "high phosphorus" starter formulation: 12-24-12, 18-24-12, or 24-25-4 type products
- DO NOT recommend Scotts 24-25-4 alongside framing as 12-24-12 — these are different N-to-P ratios; be internally consistent
- Apply AT SEEDING (day 0 to day 3 maximum)
- Light irrigation to incorporate, but seed bed must stay moist during germination
